# Indian Horse Story Map

An interactive visual story map for Richard Wagamese's novel "Indian Horse" - exploring themes of cultural genocide, identity, healing, and resilience through the lens of hockey and Indigenous experience.

## 🏒 About the Novel

"Indian Horse" is a powerful novel by Richard Wagamese that tells the story of Saul Indian Horse, an Ojibwe boy who survives the trauma of residential school and finds solace in hockey, only to face new challenges as he navigates racism and identity in the sport he loves.

## 📚 Features

- **Interactive Timeline**: Click through the five major parts of the story
- **Character Arc Visualization**: Follow Saul's journey from innocent child to integrated individual
- **Thematic Analysis**: Explore major themes including cultural genocide, identity, racism, and healing
- **Symbolic Elements**: Understand the deeper meanings behind hockey, the land, and storytelling
- **Historical Context**: Learn about the residential school system and its lasting impacts
- **Responsive Design**: Works seamlessly on desktop, tablet, and mobile devices

## 🎯 Educational Purpose

This story map is designed for:
- Students studying Indigenous literature
- Educators teaching about residential schools and their impacts
- Book clubs and reading groups
- Anyone interested in understanding the historical and cultural context of the novel

## 📚 Educational Resources

### About the Author
Richard Wagamese (1955-2017) was an Ojibwe author from the Wabaseemoong Independent Nations in Ontario, Canada. He was a prominent voice in Indigenous literature and journalism.

### Key Themes Explored

1. **Cultural Genocide**: The systematic destruction of Indigenous culture through residential schools
2. **Identity Crisis**: The struggle to maintain Indigenous identity in colonial systems
3. **Racism in Sports**: How athletic success doesn't shield from systemic discrimination
4. **Healing and Recovery**: The power of traditional practices and community support
5. **Intergenerational Trauma**: How historical trauma affects multiple generations

### Discussion Questions

- How does hockey serve as both salvation and burden for Saul?
- What role does storytelling play in Indigenous healing practices?
- How does the novel illustrate the concept of cultural genocide?
- What is the significance of Saul's return to his community?
